Delhi Style Matar Chaat Recipe - Spicy & Tangy Green Peas Chaat

Make this lip smacking Delhi style matar chat for an evening snack during the monsoons or a even for a weeknight dinner. All it needs is a little planning to make this classic dish. You can also serve it along with Kulcha or Puri for a weekend dinner along with a glass of lassi.Delhi Style Matar Chaat Recipe is a very famous street food from Delhi. It is a semi gravy dish made from dried peas cooked along with well combined spicy green gravy. It is usually served by topping up with some chopped raw onions, chopped tomatoes, sev and some chopped coriander leaves. This is then served along with Kulcha or can be eaten as it is. In Delhi it is made in a large brass pot and Kept outside the shop, the curry is kept in the pot and heated in slow flame and served to people. It is a super delicious snack and must to be tried. Serve the Delhi Style Matar Chaat Recipe as it is or along with Baked Amritsari Kulcha or Puri with a glass of Rose Lassi Recipe to enjoy your Lunch meal.

    Prep in: 500 M

    Cooks in: 45 M

    Total in: 545 M

    Makes: 4 Servings

    Ingredients

    • 1 cup Dried Green Peas (Vatana), soaked for 1 hour
    • 1 inch Ginger, finely chopped
    • 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder
    • 1/2 cup Tomatoes, chopped
    • 1/2 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera)
    • 1 teaspoon Amchur (Dry Mango Powder)
    • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi)
    • 1 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf)
    • 1/2 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini)
    • 2 Bay leaves (tej patta)
    • Salt, to taste
    • Lemon, extract from lemon

    To Garnish

    • 1 Onion, finely chopped
    • 2 inch Ginger, cut into thin strips
    • 1 Green Chilli, finely chopped
    • 2 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves, finely chopped

    How to make Delhi Style Matar Chaat Recipe - Spicy & Tangy Green Peas Chaat

    1. We begin making the Delhi Style Matar Chaat Recipe by soaking the dried green peas / matar for at least 5 to 6 hours or overnight and keep it aside.
    2. Heat a pressure cooker with oil, add bay leaf, cinnamon stick, and fennel seeds. Saute till the aroma comes out.
    3. Add ginger and saut√© for a few seconds. Add tomatoes and cook until softened.
    4. Finally, add the rest of the spice powders and give the masala for the chaat a stir. Cook until the raw smell goes away, about 3 to 4 minutes.
    5. Add the soaked dried green peas and stir. Add 2 cups of water and pressure cook for 4 to 5 whistles or for at least 20 to 25 minutes. Turn off the heat.
    6. Once done, release the pressure naturally, as the Matar Chaat will continue to cook while there is pressure.
    7. Check the taste, add lemon juice and stir. Transfer the Delhi Style Matar Chaat Recipe to small serving bowls.
    8. Sprinkle onions, ginger, green chilies, drizzle the mint chutney, and top it up with chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot.
    9. Serve the Delhi Style Matar Chaat Recipe as it is or along with Baked Amritsari Kulcha or Puri with a glass of Rose Lassi Recipe to enjoy your lunch meal.
